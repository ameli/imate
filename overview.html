Overview#

What imate Does#

The main functionality of imate is to estimate the quantity

(1)#\[ \mathrm{trace} \left( f(\mathbf{A}) \right),\]

where \(\mathbf{A}\) is a square matrix and \(f\) is a matrix function. imate can also compute variants of (1), such as

\[\mathrm{trace} \left(f(\mathbf{A}) \mathbf{B} \right),\]

and

\[\mathrm{trace} \left(f(\mathbf{A}) \mathbf{B} f(\mathbf{A}) \mathbf{C} \right).\]

Common Matrix Functions#

Some examples of \(f\) in (1) leads to the following common applications:

Log-Determinant#

If \(f: x \mapsto \log \vert x \vert\), then \(\mathrm{trace} \left(f(\mathbf{A}) \right) = \log \vert \det \mathbf{A} \vert\) is the log-determinant of \(\mathbf{A}\) which frequently appears in statistics and machine leanring.

Trace of Matrix Powers#

If \(f: x \mapsto x^p\), \(p \in \mathbb{R}\), then (1) is \(\mathrm{trace} (\mathbf{A}^p)\). This can include the nagative powers, such as the trace of inverse, \(\mathrm{trace} (\mathbf{A}^{-1})\). This function frequenity appears in statistics and machine leanring.

Schatten \(p\)-norm and Schatten \(p\)-anti-norm#

If \(f: x \mapsto x^{\frac{p}{2}}\), then \(\mathrm{trace} (\mathbf{A}^{\frac{p}{2}})\) is the Schatten \(p\)-norm (if \(p > 0\)), and is the Schatten \(p\)-anti-norm (if \(p < 0\)).

Eigencount and Numerical Rank#

If \(f: x \mapsto \mathbf{1}_{[a,b]}(x)\) is the indicator (step) function in the interval \([a, b]\), then \(\mathrm{trace}(\mathbf{1}(\mathbf{A}))\) estimates number of eigenvalues of \(\mathbf{A}\) in the latter interval, which is an inexpensive method of estimating the rank of a large matrix.

Extrada Index of Graphs#

If \(f: x \mapsto \exp(x)\), then \(\mathrm{trace} \left( f(\mathbf{A}) \right)\) is the Estrada index of \(\mathbf{A}\), which has many applications in computational biology.

Spectral Density#

if \(f: x \mapsto \delta(x - \lambda)\), where \(\delta(x)\) is the Dirac’s delta function, then \(\mathrm{trace} \left( f(\mathbf{A})\right)\) obtains the spectral density of the eigenvalues of \(\mathbf{A}\).

How imate Computes#

It is assumed that

  • Matrices are very large.

  • \(f(\mathbf{A})\) cannot be computed directly, or only known implicitly.

Because of these, instead of computing these quantities directly, imate employs randomized (stochastic) algorithms to estimate these quantities using Monte-Carlo sampling, such as by

  • Hutchinson’s method,

  • Stochastic Lanczos Quadrature method.

Such class of methods are very fast and scalable to massive matrices.

Interpolators#

A novel feature of imate is the interpolation of the above quantities when the matrix is a one-parameter affine operator

\[\mathbf{A}(t): t \mapsto \mathbf{A} + t \mathbf{B}.\]

In such cases, imate can interpolate \(\mathrm{trace} f(\mathbf{A}(t))\) for a large logarithmic range of the parameter \(t\). This novel method is very useful in optimization of hyperparameters of models with such affine matrix formulations.

Applications#

Trace of matrix functions are ubiquitous in machine learning applications, and in fact, they are the most computationally expensive mathematical terms in such applications.

Take for instance the \(n\)-dimensional normal distribution \(\boldsymbol{y} \sim \mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{\mu}, \boldsymbol{\Sigma})\) by

\[p(\boldsymbol{y} \vert \boldsymbol{\mu}, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(2 \pi)^n}} \vert \boldsymbol{\Sigma} \vert^{-\frac{1}{2}} \exp \left(-\frac{1}{2} (\boldsymbol{y} - \boldsymbol{\mu})^{\intercal} \boldsymbol{\Sigma}^{-1} (\boldsymbol{y} - \boldsymbol{\mu}) \right)\]
